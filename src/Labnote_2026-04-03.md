# Sepsis Experiment Notes

## Date
- 2026-04-03

## 1. Research Objective
- Research Objective: evaluate single-model LSTM for first sepsis onset in next 6 hours using 24-hour history and document bootstrap performance.

## 2. Data Source
- PhysioNet Challenge 2019 (public ICU challenge training data)
- URL root: https://physionet.org/files/challenge-2019/1.0.0/training/
- Folders used: training_setA and training_setB
- Local data path: data/raw

## 3. Research Background and Theoretical Basis
- Sepsis is acute life-threatening response to infection; early detection is critical.
- Physiological vitals from PhysioNet provide temporal signals with missingness patterns.
- LSTM is the next sequential baseline in the proposal because it can model temporal order directly rather than relying on flattened feature windows.

## 4. Experimental Procedures
- Preprocess: read p*.psv files, causal forward fill, train-set median imputation for remaining NaNs, z-score normalization with train statistics.
- Features: HR, O2Sat, Temp, SBP, MAP, DBP, Resp, FiO2
- Label: 1 if first sepsis onset occurs within (t, t+6] after 24h history window.
- Split: 70/15/15 sequence-based (with 1867/400/401 samples from 2668 total windows).
- Model: 1-layer LSTM(hidden_size=64) with final-step linear head.
- Training: BCEWithLogitsLoss with positive-class weighting, Adam optimizer, batch_size=128, epochs=8, learning_rate=0.001.
- Model selection: best epoch by validation AUROC.
- Bootstrap metrics on test: 100 rounds.

## 6. Analysis and Conclusion
- Conclusion: the LSTM materially improved ranking discrimination over logistic regression and gradient-boosted trees on this subset, but it achieved that by predicting many positives at threshold 0.5, producing perfect sensitivity with weak specificity.
- Interpretation: the model appears useful as a temporal detector, yet its probability estimates are poorly calibrated, as reflected by the very high Brier score.
- Limitations: only 220 patients, sequence-based split instead of patient-level split, fixed threshold=0.5, and no calibration or regularization sweep.
- Next steps: tune threshold on validation data, add dropout/early stopping, evaluate calibration methods, and repeat on a patient-level split.
